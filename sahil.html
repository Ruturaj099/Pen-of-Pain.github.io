The Pulse Behind Silence

 Sahil’s world thrived in frequencies and wires.
At just 22, he had become a name whispered with respect in the corners of Yavatmal’s tech world—especially among theatre owners, where he resurrected dying amplifiers like a wizard of sound. Brilliant in studies, humble in nature, and unusually gifted in electronics, Sahil balanced his diploma in Electronics and Telecommunication with Sunday repair gigs to support his family.

But despite all this, he was never proud—never arrogant. If you walked into his college, you'd hear his name not in awe, but in gratitude. “Sahil helped me with the project,” “He stayed late to teach me,” “I passed because of him.” That’s just who he was.

But in every light, there is a shadow.

Chanesh.
Sharp as a scalpel, first ranker of the class—but as cold as a broken circuit. He never helped, never shared, never smiled. Knowledgeable in books, but clueless in real-world tech. While Sahil’s reputation soared like a symphony, Chanesh simmered in silent rage. The topper... outshined by someone he considered "just a technician."

Then came the event.

The city’s biggest multiplex was due for a tech upgrade. Their ancient sound system had crashed a week before the Yavatmal Film Festival. The owner, frantic, called for Sahil. “You’ve done it before,” he begged. “You’re our last hope.”

Sahil agreed, though it meant skipping a major internal exam. He worked day and night, sleeping on the dusty carpet of the dark theatre, surrounded by open boards and humming wires. And slowly… the silence broke. The amplifiers roared again. The system was not just repaired—it sounded alive.

The news spread like wildfire. The principal called Sahil on stage during the tech symposium, praised his genius, and said, “This is what real knowledge looks like—application, not memorization.”

Chanesh burned.

And then, the sabotage began.

A week later, Sahil submitted his final year project—a noise-cancellation system built from scrap parts. But when the panel tested it, nothing worked. It was dead. The board inside had been tampered with. Screws were swapped, a chip melted.

His reputation took a hit. Whispers began—Maybe he’s just lucky. Maybe he’s not that brilliant.

Sahil didn’t speak. He just went home… and rebuilt the project overnight.

The next day, in front of the entire panel, he demonstrated the device again. Not only did it work, it outperformed every other project. The silence that followed was louder than applause.

And then… Sahil did something no one expected.

He walked to the mic and said, “I want to thank the one who sabotaged my project. Because of you, I found a better version of myself.”

Chanesh’s face drained of color. People connected the dots. No proof, no accusations—but sometimes, silence is the loudest confession.

Months later, Sahil walked into his first job interview at a reputed electronics firm. They said, “We’ve heard about your work with the theatre. Welcome aboard.”

And Chanesh? He had the marks… but no one to vouch for his character. He faded into the background of a world that values more than just scores.
